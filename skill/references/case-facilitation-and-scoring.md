# Case Facilitation & Scoring — Run a Case, Grade It, Coach It

How to **run** a case as the interviewer, **score** it on a consistent rubric, and **coach** the gap — plus a deliberate-practice loop and case-log template for getting better over repetitions. This is the operational layer that turns the case banks (`case-bank-interview-classics.md`, `case-bank-worked.md`, `practice-cases-quantified.md`) into live, gradeable practice. Distilled from public MBA casebook "how to give a case" and evaluation sections into native form.

Use it when: acting as an interviewer/coach, scoring a candidate (or yourself), running a mock, or building a structured practice plan. When the user asks Claude to "run me through a case" or "score my structure," this is the operating manual.

---

## Formats: know which case you're running

| Format | Who drives | You (interviewer) do | Best for testing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Candidate-led** (BCG/Bain-style) | The candidate | Give a lean prompt, answer only what's asked, release exhibits on request | Initiative, prioritisation, ownership |
| **Interviewer-led** (McKinsey-style) | You | Walk them through pre-set questions in sequence, feed data at each | Depth on each step, follow-direction, quant |
| **Collaborative / conversational** | Shared | Treat it as a working session; nudge when stuck | Rapport, real-work feel |
| **Written / group (GD)** | Varies | Give a pack or a group prompt; observe structuring and synthesis | Prioritisation under time; teaming |

**Golden rule of facilitation:** give the candidate only the **prompt** first. Release each data point **when they ask for the right node** — never volunteer the answer. If they ask a vague question ("tell me about costs"), make them sharpen it. The information you *make them earn* is the information you can grade.

## How to run a case, end to end

1. **Set the prompt.** One or two sentences: client, situation, the question. State the objective plainly; hold back scope details for them to clarify.
2. **Let them clarify.** Reward 2–4 sharp questions (objective, business model, scope, success metric). Penalise diving in with no clarification — or ten scattershot ones.
3. **Take the structure.** Give ~90 seconds of silence. Then have them **walk you through it out loud**. Grade MECE, tailoring, prioritisation, and whether they lead with a hypothesis (see rubric).
4. **Drive the branches.** Follow their lead (candidate-led) or your sequence (interviewer-led). When they ask for data on the right node, hand it over. Keep a light "are we still answering the question?" pressure.
5. **Insert the exhibit.** Hand over one chart at the natural moment. Grade: do they read title/axes, state the insight, quantify the gap, and tie it to the question? (Protocol in `case-cracking-drills.md` Part B.)
6. **Force the math.** Make them set up the approach before computing, and **demand the "so what"** after every number. A correct number with no interpretation is a partial miss.
7. **Ask for the recommendation.** Give a "the CEO just walked in — what do you recommend?" prompt. Expect: **answer first**, 2–3 supporting reasons, risks, and a next step. Top-down, under 60–90 seconds.
8. **Pressure-test.** One or two challenges ("why not the opposite?", "what would change your mind?"). Grade composure and whether they defend with logic vs. crumble or dig in blindly.

## The scoring rubric (five dimensions, 1–4 each)

Score each dimension 1 (weak) to 4 (strong). Elite candidates aren't perfect everywhere — they're 3–4 on structure, quant, and synthesis with no 1s.

### 1. Structure & framework
- ☐ MECE — no overlapping buckets, no gaps
- ☐ **Tailored** to this case ("revenue from oil-barrel sales" > "revenue"), not a bolted-on template
- ☐ Used the structure to *drive* the case, not abandoned after minute two
- ☐ Prioritised branches and stated a Day-1 hypothesis

### 2. Analytical & problem-solving (quant)
- ☐ Quick and accurate calculation; approach stated before computing
- ☐ Organised the fact base; kept a complex case structured
- ☐ **Read exhibits accurately** and drew the insight fast
- ☐ Creative in generating causes/options, not just mechanical

### 3. Synthesis & conclusion
- ☐ Conclusions stated **logically and succinctly**, answer-first
- ☐ Backed by evidence developed *during* the case
- ☐ Comprehensive — captured the relevant points, answered the actual question
- ☐ "So what" present on each number and chart

### 4. Communication
- ☐ Verbalised thinking at every stage; no long silent gaps
- ☐ Crisp and concise — no rambling or wandering
- ☐ Built rapport — a conversation, not a monologue
- ☐ Signposted ("three things: first… second… third…")

### 5. Presence & drive
- ☐ **Drove** the case — active, not passive
- ☐ Confident and composed, including under challenge and after a mistake
- ☐ Engaged and enthusiastic
- ☐ Recovered from errors with poise

**Rolling it up (interview-classic style):** an overall read plus two sub-scores that best predict performance — **Quant** and **Creativity/Structure**. A quick 1–3 tag on each (1 = strong, 3 = needs work) is enough to compare cases and track a candidate over sessions.

## Common mistakes catalogue (what to watch for and coach)

**Structuring**
- Slapping on a memorised framework regardless of fit ("Porter's for a profitability case").
- Overlapping or non-exhaustive buckets; no prioritisation; no hypothesis.
- Reading the structure silently off the page instead of presenting it.

**During the case**
- Not driving — waiting to be led when the format is candidate-led.
- Diving into computation without stating the approach.
- Getting lost in one branch and forgetting the overall question.
- Asking vague data questions ("what about the market?") instead of targeted ones.

**Exhibits & math**
- Describing a chart instead of extracting its insight; missing the outlier.
- Misreading axes / truncated scales; no quantification ("much bigger").
- A number with no "so what"; not sanity-checking an implausible result.

**Synthesis & communication**
- Presenting analysis instead of an answer; burying the recommendation.
- Rambling; no signposting; monologuing without engaging the interviewer.
- Crumbling under a challenge — or the opposite, defending a wrong point stubbornly.

**Fit** (see `fit-pei-drill-bank.md`)
- "We" instead of "I"; no quantified result; humble-brag "failures"; brochure "why firm" answers.

## The deliberate-practice loop

Volume alone doesn't build skill — **targeted repetition on your weakest dimension** does. Budget ~60–80 hours of prep for a full recruiting cycle; most of it should be live cases with feedback, not passive reading.

```
1. CASE   run one case (as candidate or interviewer)
2. SCORE  1–4 on all five dimensions, immediately, honestly
3. DIAGNOSE  find the single lowest dimension
4. DRILL  isolate it — e.g. if quant is the gap, do 10 math drills;
          if structure, do structuring drills; if exhibits, chart drills
5. RE-RUN a fresh case and re-score the same dimension
   → repeat; rotate case types so breadth grows alongside depth
```

Use `case-cracking-drills.md` for the isolated math/exhibit/structuring reps and `case-bank-interview-classics.md` / `case-bank-worked.md` for the full cases. Rotate types (profitability, entry, M&A, growth, pricing, sizing, non-traditional) so no archetype is unfamiliar on interview day.

## Case log template (track every rep)

Keep a running log — it turns scattered practice into a visible skill curve and reveals which case *types* and *dimensions* lag.

| Date | Case (name / type / industry) | Format | Partner | Structure | Quant | Synthesis | Comm | Presence | Weakest → drill next |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| — | Knight Perfumes / profitability / CPG | interviewer-led | — | 3 | 2 | 3 | 3 | 3 | Quant → 10 margin-bridge drills |
| — | Army Hotel / market entry / hospitality | candidate-led | — | 3 | 3 | 2 | 3 | 3 | Synthesis → answer-first reps |

**Review cadence:** every 5–8 cases, scan the log for the lowest recurring dimension and the least-practised case type, and aim the next block there. The goal isn't a perfect score every time — it's no 1s, rising quant/structure/synthesis, and full type coverage before the real thing.
